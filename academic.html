Master's Thesis Virtual Reality PTSD Treatment Adaptive Systems

Tailored Immersion: Adaptive Virtual Reality as a Tool for Personalized PTSD Treatment

Uppsala University — MSSc Human-Computer Interaction (Filosofie masterexamen) — 2024

This thesis looks at how VR exposure therapy can adapt to the individual patient rather than following a fixed protocol. Gradual exposure is a well-established approach to PTSD treatment, but most existing VR implementations are static — same sequence, same pacing, regardless of how the person in front of you is actually responding.

The work proposes a design model for adaptive VR therapy built around a 10-level gradual exposure system that adjusts based on behavioural and physiological cues, paired with a therapist-facing interface that gives clinicians real visibility into the session without getting in the way of the therapeutic relationship. The central argument is that personalisation here isn't a nice-to-have — it's what makes the tool clinically usable.

Key contributions

  • 10-level gradual exposure system with adaptive parameterisation
  • Therapist-facing control interface for real-time session management
  • Design framework for clinically-grounded adaptive VR
  • User-centred methodology with clinical stakeholders throughout
  • Analysis of the tension between automation and therapist agency
Bachelor's Thesis Assistive Technology User-Centred Design

Increased Quality of Life for Physically Disabled Individuals: Factors in Welfare Technology Development

University of Skövde — BSc Informatics — 2022

This thesis examines what actually needs to be considered when designing assistive technology — not just the technical requirements, but the contextual, social, and user-centred factors that often get underweighted in practice.

The work looks at how assistive tech gets designed, what tends to go wrong, and what a more grounded approach looks like — one that takes seriously the complexity of the users and use contexts involved.

Key contributions

  • Analysis of key design factors in assistive technology development
  • Framework for user-centred AT design grounded in real-world context
  • Examination of where standard development approaches fall short for this population
