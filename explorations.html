Current interest

Animal-Computer Interaction

I came across ACI not that long ago and it stuck with me. The design problem is genuinely interesting: how do you build technology for users who experience the world completely differently from you, and who can't tell you when you've got it wrong? That's a hard constraint to design around. I'm early in this. Reading, thinking, sketching things out.

Some of those sketches are below.

I have a cat that will crawl into her hut and bite you if you reach in, even though she never bites otherwise. That kind of behavior stuck with me. And then I read this paper about animal privacy paper by Paci et al (2022). And it might seem obvious for the reader, but she chose that space, and the enclosure clearly means something to her. Maybe the bite signified her discomfort and need for privacy. It got me thinking about whether technology could actually support that, rather than just provide a static box and call it a day.

The concept I sketched out is a system that amplifies what the cat is already communicating. If she enters the hut and settles, grass-like barriers around the entrance gradually rise, reinforcing the concealment she already chose. The flowchart below maps the interaction logic, including how posture is read through sensors and how the system immediately hands control back the moment she shows any sign of discomfort. It's early thinking and raises more questions than it answers, but that felt like the point.
