Current interest

Animal-Computer Interaction

I came across ACI not that long ago and it stuck with me. The design problem is genuinely interesting: how do you make technology for users who experience the world completely differently from you, and who can't give you feedback the way a human user can? That's a hard constraint to design around. I'm early in this — reading, thinking. Not much more than that yet.

One thing I keep coming back to is the question of data and consent. If a system is collecting behavioural signals from an animal — what it does, how it moves, what it responds to — who does that data belong to? What would it even mean to design a system that took the animal's interests seriously at that level, rather than just using the data for human ends?

I started mapping this out. The flowchart below is an early attempt to think through how data collected about a cat might flow, who controls it, and where the ethical questions actually sit. It's rough — more a way of making the problem visible than a finished argument.
